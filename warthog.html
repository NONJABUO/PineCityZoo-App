WARTHOG

Phacochoerus is a genus in the family Suidae, commonly known as warthogs (pronounced wart-hog).

Although covered in bristly hairs, a warthog's body and head appear largely bare, from a distance, with only a crest of hair along the back and the tufts on the face and tail being obvious.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM?

They are pigs who live in open and semi-open habitats, even in quite arid regions, in sub-Saharan Africa.
